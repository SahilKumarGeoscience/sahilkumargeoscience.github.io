## Research Snapshot

I am a geoscience graduate researcher working on numerical landscape evolution modelling, geomorphology, and long-term surface stability. My master’s thesis investigates future landscape evolution of the Swabian Alb over a 1 Myr timescale, with a focus on lithological control of erosion hotspots, plateau stability, escarpment retreat, and drainage-divide migration.

The EGU 2026 poster presents results from 1,728 forward model simulations using Landlab-based landscape evolution modelling. The main finding is that high-magnitude erosion remains concentrated along the Albtrauf escarpment, while the plateau interior remains comparatively stable. Lithology is the dominant control on the spatial erosion pattern, while climate mainly amplifies incision where active channels are already present.
